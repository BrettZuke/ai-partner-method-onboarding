# Onboard Creator Client

## Goal
Take a newly signed creator client from "deal closed" to "systems live" within 7 days. Make them feel like they joined something premium and organised from the first message.

## Inputs
- Signed agreement / payment confirmed
- Client name, email, Instagram handle

## Tools
- `execution/create_typeform.py` — generates your intake form
- Notion — client workspace (see `notion-template/client-workspace.md`)
- Your booking link (Calendly / iClosed)

---

## The Process

### Step 0 — Deal Closed (Day 0, within 1 hour)

The moment payment clears, do these four things:

**1. Send the welcome message** (DM or WhatsApp):

> "Welcome to the team [Name]. Genuinely excited to build this with you.
>
> I'm sending you an onboarding form now — set aside 20–30 minutes and fill it in properly. Everything we build for you comes from your answers, so the more detail the better.
>
> You've got 48 hours to complete it. Here's the link: [TYPEFORM URL]
>
> Once that's done I'll be in touch to book our kickoff call."

**2. Create their Notion workspace** — follow `notion-template/client-workspace.md` exactly. Takes ~20 minutes.

**3. Share the Notion workspace** — send them the link with view access.

**4. Add to your CRM** — status: Active Client. Note the deal date and 48hr form deadline.

---

### Step 1 — Form Completed (Day 1–2)

Wait for the Typeform submission. You'll get an email notification.

If they haven't submitted after 48 hours, send one chase:

> "Hey [Name] — just checking you got the form link okay? Wanted to make sure it didn't end up in spam. Here it is again: [LINK]. Let me know if you have any issues."

If still nothing after 72 hours, get them on a call and complete it together. Do not build systems on missing data.

---

### Step 2 — Review Answers (Before Kickoff Call)

Read every answer carefully before the call. You're looking for:

**Green flags — build with confidence:**
- ICP is specific enough to write DM qualifiers from
- Brand voice is genuinely distinctive, not generic
- Competitors list makes sense for their niche
- Offer price matches their audience

**Red flags — address on the kickoff call:**
- ICP description is too vague ("anyone who wants to make money")
- Brand voice is corporate or AI-sounding
- No content posted, no existing proof of concept
- Offer is unclear or underpriced for what they're promising
- Revenue goal is wildly unrealistic given where they're starting

**Things to prepare based on their answers:**
- Draft 3 DM qualifier questions for their Settoku setup
- Note the 2–3 content pillars from their brand voice + competitors
- Identify the one biggest bottleneck to attack first

---

### Step 3 — Kickoff Call (Day 2–3, 45 minutes)

**Agenda:**

| Time | Topic |
|------|-------|
| 0–10 min | Confirm offer + ICP — make sure what they wrote matches what they actually sell and who they sell to |
| 10–20 min | Set 30/60/90 targets — be specific: X clients, £X revenue, X DMs/day. Write these in their Notion roadmap during the call |
| 20–30 min | Map Week 1 — what gets built first, in what order |
| 30–35 min | Access + logins — confirm they've shared everything needed to build |
| 35–45 min | Expectations — response times, content filming schedule, weekly check-in format |

**End of call, confirm:**
- [ ] 90-day targets agreed and written down
- [ ] Week 1 deliverables agreed
- [ ] They know what they need to film this week
- [ ] You have all access you need
- [ ] Next check-in booked

---

### Step 4 — Technical Setup (Week 1)

Work through this in order:

**Day 1:**
- [ ] IG access connected and tested
- [ ] DM setter (Settoku) configured — brand voice prompt, ICP qualifiers, opener sequence
- [ ] First DM sequence written and sent to client for approval

**Day 2:**
- [ ] Content brief #1 delivered (based on brand voice + best-performing content from their form)
- [ ] Notion Brand Sheet populated from their Typeform answers
- [ ] Software Logins page filled in

**Day 3–5:**
- [ ] DM setter live and running
- [ ] Client has filmed content based on brief #1
- [ ] Posting schedule agreed and in the Content Kanban

---

### Step 5 — End of Week 1 Check-in

Quick 15-minute call or voice note review:

- Is the DM setter firing correctly?
- Did they film content?
- Any blockers?
- Confirm Week 2 plan

**By end of Week 1, the client should have:**
- Live DM setter running on their IG
- First content brief in their hands
- Notion workspace fully populated
- First piece of content filmed or scheduled

---

## Edge Cases

**Client misses the 48hr form deadline**
Chase once. If still nothing at 72hrs, get on a call and complete it together. Never build on missing data.

**Form answers are too vague**
Don't guess. Bring the specific vague answers to the kickoff call and work through them live. Update the form submission notes in their Notion Brand Sheet with what you clarified.

**Client has existing tools that conflict with what you're building**
Map what stays, what gets replaced, document clearly in their Software Logins page. Never rip out a tool without confirming they want it gone.

**Client goes quiet after signing**
24hr chase → 48hr chase → escalate to a call. If still nothing after 5 days, flag it. Silent clients don't get results.

**Client wants to change their offer mid-onboarding**
Pause setup. Get on a call. Nail the offer first. Building systems on a moving offer wastes everyone's time.
